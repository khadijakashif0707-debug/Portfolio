Buttr

Branding Mascot Bakery Packaging
Project — Buttr Butterbread
Year — 2026
Scope — Identity, Mascot, Packaging, Collateral
Role — Brand & Visual Designer

Buttr began with a simple question: what does comfort actually look like?

Every neighbourhood has a place people return to without quite knowing why — where the smell of butter and yeast hits before the door does, and five minutes turns into forty. Buttr is that place, built into a brand. The name itself is a small piece of design: warm, round, a little playful, melting the extra vowel the way butter melts into fresh bread.

At the centre of the identity is a mascot with no hard edges — a soft, doughy character that turns up on cups, packaging, and signage doing exactly what you'd want a fresh bake to do: sit there looking pleased with itself. Paired with a rounded, confident wordmark and a palette pulled straight from the bakery case — toasted terracotta, warm cream, golden brown — the system was built to feel less like branding and more like something you'd want to hold onto, fold into a tote bag, or wear home.

The goal wasn't just recognition. It was to give people a reason to feel something the moment they see the logo — a little warmth, a little familiarity, the sense that someone thought about their morning before they even walked in.
